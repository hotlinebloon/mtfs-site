# Product

## Register

Mapping the Future with STEM

## Short Name

MTFS

## Users

MTFS speaks to a mixed public audience: students who may join programs, young people who may volunteer, parents and educators evaluating credibility, partner organizations, sponsors, and community members learning what the organization does. New visitors should quickly understand that MTFS is serious, youth-led, capable, and welcoming enough to feel like they can become part of the family.

## Product Purpose

The website is the complete marketing presence for Mapping the Future with STEM. It should make the organization known, explain the work clearly, and build trust without trying to list every activity ever completed. Success means a visitor can understand the brand, see the quality of the work, and know where to go next: join, partner, sponsor, or explore projects.

## Brand Personality

Serious, youth-led, capable, and community-minded. The voice should feel professional and clean while still carrying warmth. MTFS should sound like a focused youth organization doing real STEM and civic work, not like a generic tech startup or a loose school club.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic tech-startup patterns: vague innovation copy, empty claims, decorative gradients, inflated metrics, template sections, and startup buzzwords. Avoid childish school-club styling, corporate NGO stiffness, and random homepage copy that is not grounded in actual MTFS work.

## Design Principles

- Lead with credibility: make the organization feel serious before asking visitors to act.
- Keep the story focused: show selected work and clear pathways instead of trying to include everything.
- Make belonging visible: new students and volunteers should feel invited into a real community.
- Say only what is true: homepage and page copy must be based on actual MTFS programs, people, and outcomes.
- Stay clean without becoming generic: use restraint, hierarchy, and brand-specific details rather than startup templates.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA as the baseline. Maintain readable contrast, keyboard-accessible navigation and actions, visible focus states, reduced-motion support, and responsive layouts that keep content readable on mobile and desktop.
